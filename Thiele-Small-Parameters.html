    Thiele-Small Parameters

    Thiele-Small Parameters

    May 22, 20243 min read

    Or T/S Parameters

    F

    Fs​ - driver resonance frequency any speaker will have a hard time to play any frequency below the Fs​

    fs​=2πCms​⋅Mms​​1​

    F0​ - The frequency when frequency response start to drop F3​ - frequency where the 3 decibel power drop occurs, less than this you will feel that the sound is quieter Fb​ - box resonance frequency

    Q

    Q - Quantity factor, the magnification of resonance factor of any resonant device or circuit. A driver with a high Q is more resonant than one with a low Q.

    Q can also be called as “damping” lower Q will have more damping (more damping is more clear more good things)

    Qms​ - the mechanical Q of the driver. (Spring from surround and spider)

    • Higher Qms​ drivers displaying a higher impedance peak

    Qes​ - the electrical Q of the driver. This causes by Back EMF (same as motor)

    Qts​ - the total Q of the driver at Fs.

    Qts​1​=Qms​1​+Qes​1​

    Qts​=Qes​+Qms​Qes​⋅Qms​​

    Finding Qms​ and Qes​

    Zmax​ = impedance at Fs​ Z0​=Re​×Zmax​​ find F1​ and F2​ in impedance curve (F1​ and F2​ have difference meaning from F3​) Qms​=F2​−F1​Fs​​×Re​Zmax​​​

     Qms = Fs/(F2 - F1)*sqrt(Zmax/Re)

    Qes​=Re​Zmax​​−1Qms​​ Qes​=F2​−F1​Fs​​×Zmax​−Re​Zmax​⋅Re​​​

    Qes = Qms/(Zmax/Re - 1)

    Relation with other variables

    Qes​=(Bl)22π⋅fs​⋅Mms​⋅Re​​=(Bl)2Re​​Cms​Mms​​​ Qms​=Rms​2π⋅fs​⋅Mms​​=Rms​1​Cms​Mms​​​

    ref. Measuring transducer Qms, Qes, Qts (wavecor.com)

    Cms​ - Compliance Higher (loose suspension) → yield a lower frequency Lower (stiffer suspension) → yield a higher frequency

    Re​ - DC resistance (normally 30% less than impedance) Impedance, Z - AC resistance

    Bl - Magnetic force factor / motor strength (T⋅Nm)

    B×l. Which is (the flux density) x (the length of the voice coil)

    Or, actually

    Bl=B×lsinθ θ=90°,sin(90°)=1

    So,

    Bl=B×l

    Relative to other parameters

    Qes=Bl22π⋅Fs​⋅Mms⋅Re​​ Bl=Qes2π⋅Fs​⋅Mms⋅Re​​​

    Dimensions

    V

    Vas - air volume equal equal to compliance of driver suspension (springiness of driver) = Cms​ describe in volume

    Vas​=ρ⋅c2⋅Sd2​⋅Cms​

    Vb - Net enclosure internal volume

    Xmax - maximum distance that coin can travel without distortion (they said that travel of 115% Xmax will not introduce audible distortion)

    In 5-7” speaker Xmax should be more than 3mm In 8-12” speaker Xmax should be more than 6mm

    Xmech - maximum distance a speaker can travel without damaging the driver

    >Xmax = Distortion >Xmech = Damage!!

    Sd - effective area of the cone (πr2) more Sd = more bass less Sd = more spread high frequency

    Mmd = moving mass (mass of Coil + Cone + spider/2 + surround/2) Mms = Mmd + mass of air in front of the cone

    High Mmd → Low Fs​ Low Mmd → High Fs​

    SPL

    SPL

    units

    • dB@W/m
    • dB@2.83V/m

    Difference driver Impedance will effect dB@2.83V/m unit

    W​=V2/Z=2.832/8=8/8=1​ dB\;@\;2.83V/m\;@\;8\ohm &= \pm 0\;dB\;@\;W/m \\ dB\;@\;2.83V/m\;@\;6\ohm &= -1.3\;dB\;@\;W/m \\ dB\;@\;2.83V/m\;@\;4\ohm &= -3\;dB\;@\;W/m \\ dB\;@\;2.83V/m\;@\;2\ohm &= -6\;dB\;@\;W/m \\ \end{split}

    twice the power give +3 dB half the power give -3 dB one tenth the power give -10 dB

    ΔdB=10log(P2​P1​​)

    Ex

    1W2W4W8W10W16W​⇒⇒⇒⇒⇒⇒​90dB93dB96dB99dB100dB102dB​

    Max SPL

    MaxSPL=Efficiency@W/m+10×log(MaxPower)

    Perceived loudness

    • 3 dB difference is difficult to hear
    • 10 dB considered to be twice as loud
